# Code of Conduct

## Our adopted standard

The deEHR project adopts the **Contributor Covenant, version 2.1** as its Code
of Conduct. The Contributor Covenant is a widely used community standard for
open-source projects.

The full text is published here:
<https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/>

All participants — contributors, maintainers, reviewers and community members —
are expected to follow it in every project space.

## Our pledge

We are committed to making participation in deEHR a welcoming, respectful and
harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible
or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and
expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status,
nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and
orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming,
diverse, inclusive and healthy community. We expect everyone to be respectful,
considerate and constructive, to assume good faith, to give and gracefully
accept feedback, and to focus on what is best for the community and for the
patients this project ultimately serves.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces — the repository,
issues, pull requests, discussions and any other project channel — and also
applies when an individual is officially representing the project in public
spaces.

## Reporting and enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behavior may be reported to the project maintainers
at **<brunocampos.ssa@gmail.com>**.

All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. The
maintainers are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the reporter
of any incident, and will keep the details of a report confidential.

Maintainers who do not follow or enforce this Code of Conduct in good faith may
face temporary or permanent consequences as determined by other members of the
project's leadership.

## Enforcement guidelines

When determining the consequences for any action judged to be in breach of this
Code of Conduct, maintainers follow the **Community Impact Guidelines** defined
in the Contributor Covenant 2.1. These describe a graduated set of responses —
ranging from a private correction, to a warning, to a temporary restriction, to
a permanent ban — proportionate to the nature and severity of the breach. See
the linked Contributor Covenant text for the full guidelines.
